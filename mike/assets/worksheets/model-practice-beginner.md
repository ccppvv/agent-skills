## Exercise 1: Model Recognition

**Goal**: Practice identifying which mental models apply to real situations.

For each scenario, identify 2-3 relevant mental models:

### Scenario A: The Startup Decision
"Sarah is offered a job at a promising startup with equity, but it pays 30% less than her current stable corporate job. The startup is in an exciting industry, but most startups fail."

**Which models apply?**
1. [Your answer]
2. [Your answer]
3. [Your answer]

<details>
<summary>Suggested Models</summary>

- **Opportunity Cost**: Choosing startup means giving up salary security
- **Expected Value**: Calculate probability-weighted outcomes
- **Base Rates**: 90% of startups fail - what does this tell us?
- **Optionality**: How reversible is this decision?
- **Circle of Competence**: Does she understand the industry?
</details>

### Scenario B: The Social Media Algorithm
"Instagram's algorithm shows you more posts similar to ones you've liked. Over time, users notice they only see content that aligns with their existing views."

**Which models apply?**
1. [Your answer]
2. [Your answer]
3. [Your answer]

<details>
<summary>Suggested Models</summary>

- **Confirmation Bias**: System reinforces existing beliefs
- **Feedback Loop**: Reinforcing loop (more likes → more similar content → more likes)
- **Filter Bubble**: Information environment narrows over time
- **Availability Heuristic**: What you see frequently seems more common
</details>

---

### Scenario C: The Coffee Shop Line
"A new coffee shop opened nearby. First week, there was a huge line. Second week, still long lines. By third week, you notice the lines are gone even though the coffee is still good."

**Which models apply?**
1. [Your answer]
2. [Your answer]
3. [Your answer]

<details>
<summary>Suggested Models</summary>

- **Regression to Mean**: Extreme initial popularity normalizes
- **Supply and Demand**: Initial scarcity (low supply, high demand) equilibrates
- **Network Effects**: Initial social proof ("everyone's going!") fades
- **Availability Heuristic**: First week visibility created perceived popularity
</details>
