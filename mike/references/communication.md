# Communication & Negotiation Models

## Reciprocity
**Principle**: People feel obligated to return favors.
**Application**: Giving first creates obligation; negotiation tactics.
**Example**: Free samples; relationship building; strategic concessions.

## Commitment & Consistency
**Principle**: Once committed, people resist changing position.
**Application**: Securing small commitments first; foot-in-door technique.
**Example**: Trial periods; incremental asks; public commitments.

## Social Proof
**Principle**: People follow what others do.
**Application**: Testimonials; case studies; popularity signals.
**Example**: "Bestseller"; customer testimonials; crowded restaurants.

## Authority
**Principle**: People defer to perceived experts.
**Application**: Credentials; expertise demonstration; authoritative sources.
**Example**: Expert endorsements; certifications; institutional backing.

## Liking
**Principle**: People say yes to those they like.
**Application**: Rapport building; similarity emphasis; genuine interest.
**Example**: Finding commonalities; compliments; personal connection.

## Scarcity
**Principle**: Rare things seem more valuable.
**Application**: Limited offers; exclusivity; urgency creation.
**Example**: "Only 3 left"; time-limited offers; exclusive access.

## BATNA (Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement)
**Principle**: Know your walkaway point and alternatives.
**Application**: Negotiation preparation; power assessment.
**Example**: Job offers; vendor negotiations; salary discussions.

## ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement)
**Principle**: Range where agreement mutually beneficial.
**Application**: Finding win-win solutions; negotiation boundaries.
**Example**: Price negotiations; deal structuring; compromise finding.

## Anchoring (Negotiation)
**Principle**: First offer sets reference point.
**Application**: Making first offer strategically; price setting.
**Example**: Salary negotiations; real estate offers.

## Framing (Communication)
**Principle**: Same information presented differently gets different responses.
**Application**: Message crafting; persuasion; selling.
**Example**: "90% success" vs "10% failure"; gain vs. loss framing.

## Active Listening
**Principle**: Fully concentrating on what is being said.
**Application**: Understanding needs; building trust; gathering information.
**Example**: Reflecting back; asking clarifying questions; empathetic responses.

## Mirroring
**Principle**: Subtly mimicking behavior builds rapport.
**Application**: Relationship building; trust establishment.
**Example**: Matching energy levels; similar language; body language mirroring.

## Door-in-Face
**Principle**: Start with large request, then smaller one seems reasonable.
**Application**: Negotiation tactics; request strategy.
**Example**: Asking for big favor first; high initial price.

## Foot-in-Door  
**Principle**: Small agreement leads to larger compliance.
**Application**: Incremental commitment building.
**Example**: Free trial then upgrade; small ask before big one.

## The Ben Franklin Effect
**Principle**: Doing favor for someone makes you like them more.
**Application**: Relationship building through asking small favors.
**Example**: Asking for advice; small help requests; involvement creation.
